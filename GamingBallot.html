Introduction

In this report, we look into analyzing how people vote on gaming legalization in different US counties, using a detailed dataset of 1287 records. This data includes key information like population, income, and how many people are for or against gaming. We've applied three main machine learning techniques—Decision Trees, Naïve Bayes, and K-Nearest Neighbors—to predict voting outcomes, ensuring our approach is solid by preprocessing data and selecting relevant variables.

Prepping the data was key, as we had to make sure everything was in the right format and only included useful information for our predictions. Our goal with this analysis is to shed light on what drives voting behavior on this issue and provide valuable insights into the mix of economic, demographic, and social factors that influence these decisions. This concise journey through data analysis to prediction showcases our methodical approach to understanding gaming legalization votes in a political data science context.
